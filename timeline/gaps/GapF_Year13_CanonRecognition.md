# GAP F: YEAR 13 — CANON RECOGNITION
## Son of Cosmos: "Oh No, I'm in THAT Anime"
## Compiled December 29, 2025

---

# OVERVIEW

**Year:** 13 (April 2001 - March 2002)
**Tenchi's Age:** 16→17 (High School 2nd Year)
**Sheldon's Apparent Age:** Still ~25-27
**Time at Shrine:** 12 years completed

This is the year everything changes. After 12 years of peaceful slice-of-life, the universe Sheldon forgot he was in finally reveals itself.

---

# PART 1: THE RECOGNITION MOMENT

## 1.1 — What Sheldon "Knew"

**From His Previous Life:**
- Watched "some anime" in college (late 1990s-2000s)
- Tenchi Muyo was ONE of many
- Watched casually, not obsessively
- Remembers: space pirates, shrine, harem comedy, something about goddesses?
- DOESN'T remember: specific plot details, character names, timeline

**Memory Fog (Cosmos's Design):**
- Deliberate protection
- Prevents him from "gaming" the universe
- Details surface only when triggered
- He can't deliberately remember; it has to come to him

**What He's Had for 12 Years:**
- Vague sense of familiarity (the shrine, the layout)
- Dreams that might be memories
- Déjà vu that never quite resolves
- No concrete recognition... until now

## 1.2 — The Trigger

**Date:** Summer 2001 (July-August)
**Location:** Masaki Shrine
**Circumstances:** Tenchi has been increasingly curious about the sealed cave

### The Moment:

Tenchi, age 17, finally does what teenage boys do: he ignores his grandfather's warnings and enters the forbidden cave.

Sheldon is in the kitchen when he feels it. A pulse. Energy he's never felt before — wild, ancient, ANGRY.

He drops what he's holding. Runs outside.

Yosho is already moving toward the cave, faster than an old man should.

And then —

A woman emerges. Cyan hair. Gold eyes. Energy crackling around her like a storm.

She looks at Tenchi. She looks at Yosho.

She SCREAMS.

And Sheldon's memory fog SHATTERS.

### The Flood:

In the span of three seconds, Sheldon remembers:
- "Tenchi Muyo" — the title
- Ryoko — the space pirate
- Ayeka — the princess who comes next
- Washu — the scientist in the gems
- The harem — oh god, the HAREM
- The stakes — universe-level threats incoming

He stands frozen as 12 years of "where am I?" crystallizes into horrifying clarity.

> *I'm in Tenchi Muyo.*
> *I've been in Tenchi Muyo this whole time.*
> *I've been raising the protagonist of a harem anime.*
> *And I just watched his love interest wake up.*

---

## 1.3 — Immediate Aftermath

Sheldon does NOT have time to process. Ryoko is attacking. Tenchi is in danger. Yosho is revealing his true capabilities.

**What Sheldon Does:**
- Gets Tenchi to safety (priority one, always)
- Stays out of the combat (not his role)
- Watches Yosho handle Ryoko
- Processes in fragments between crises

**The First Night:**
After Ryoko is... not defeated, but contained... stabilized... after Tenchi is checked and re-checked... after Yosho explains (partially) to his grandson...

Sheldon sits on the shrine steps at 3 AM, staring at nothing.

**His Thoughts:**
- "I watched this show. I WATCHED this show."
- "Yosho is... Yosho. The legendary prince."
- "Tenchi is THE Tenchi. The chosen one. The Light Hawk Wing wielder."
- "The cave demon... was Ryoko. The space pirate. Who becomes..."
- "Oh no."
- "The harem."
- "I've been living in a harem comedy for TWELVE YEARS and I didn't KNOW."

# PART 2: THE KNOWLEDGE PROBLEM

| **Washu** | Scientist. "Greatest in universe." Trapped in gems. | That she's a Chousin, her full scope |
| **Mihoshi** | Galaxy Police. Ditzy. Crashes nearby? | Her family significance, exact timing |
| **Sasami** | Little sister. Connected to... something. A goddess? | Tsunami bond specifics |
| **Kagato** | Main villain. Very bad. | When he attacks, how he's defeated |
| **Plot** | General arc: threats escalate, family forms | Specific episodes, order of events |

**The Memory Fog Continues:**
- He can't force recall
- Details emerge when relevant (protective measure)
- He knows ENOUGH to be terrified
- Not enough to perfectly game events

## 2.2 — The Ethical Dilemma

**What Sheldon Could Do:**
- Warn Tenchi about upcoming events
- Prepare the household for each arrival
- Try to prevent crises before they happen
- Play prophet

**What Sheldon Actually Does:**
- Almost nothing.

**Why:**
Because he's spent 12 years in this world. It's not an anime anymore. These are real people. Tenchi is his SON in every way that matters. Yosho is his... partner. Friend. The person he loves.

If he treats this like a story to be manipulated, he reduces everyone to characters.

If he plays prophet, he takes away their choices.

And besides — his memories are FRAGMENTS. What if he's wrong? What if this universe isn't exactly like the anime? What if his "knowledge" causes MORE harm than just letting events unfold?

**His Decision:**
- Don't reveal meta-knowledge
- Be present for crises
- Support the family as they navigate
- Only intervene if lives are at immediate risk and he has actionable information

---

## 2.3 — The Conversation with Yosho

**Date:** Two days after Ryoko's awakening
**Location:** Evening walk, forest path
**Mood:** Yosho knows something is wrong with Sheldon

### The Exchange:

> **YOSHO:** "You recognized her."

> **SHELDON:** "What?"

> **YOSHO:** "The demon. When she emerged. Your face. You recognized her before I said her name."

Sheldon stops walking. Of course Yosho noticed. Yosho notices everything.

> **SHELDON:** "I... yes."

> **YOSHO:** "How?"

Sheldon debates lying. Decides he can't. Not to Yosho. Not after Year 4.

> **SHELDON:** "I think... where I come from... this world is a story. A fiction. Entertainment. I watched it, once, in my previous life. I didn't remember until I saw her face."

> **YOSHO:** "You knew what would happen."

> **SHELDON:** "Fragments. Impressions. Not... not a script. More like... having read the summary on the back of a book, years ago, and trying to remember the plot."

> **YOSHO:** "Do you know what comes next?"

> **SHELDON:** "I know... more people arrive. Some as friends. Some as enemies. I know Tenchi is important. More important than I understood. I know there are... threats. But I don't know when. I don't know details. And I don't know if what I remember is even accurate here."

A long pause.

> **YOSHO:** "Will you tell Tenchi?"

> **SHELDON:** "No. He needs to be himself, not a character in a story I half-remember. He deserves to make his own choices."

Yosho nods slowly.

> **YOSHO:** "Then we continue as we have. We protect him. We guide him. We let him become who he is meant to become."

> **SHELDON:** "And if I remember something critical? Something that could save lives?"

> **YOSHO:** "Then you tell me. We decide together."

# PART 3: THE HAREM TRANSFORMATION

## 3.1 — The Core Change

**In Canon:**
- Women arrive seeking Tenchi (romantically or politically)
- Rivalry ensues
- Harem comedy dynamics
- Tenchi is passive, surrounded by competing interests

**In Son of Cosmos:**
- Women arrive to a HOUSEHOLD, not a bachelor
- Sheldon has been "dad" for 12 years
- Yosho has been active, not retired
- The dynamic shifts: not "love interests" but "joining a family"

## 3.2 — Ryoko's Arc (Altered)

**Canon Ryoko:**
- Awakens hostile
- Becomes obsessed with Tenchi
- Competes with Ayeka constantly

**Son of Cosmos Ryoko:**
- Awakens hostile (same)
- Encounters a household, not a lonely boy
- Sheldon treats her with... kindness?
- Confusion: Why isn't he afraid? Why is he offering her FOOD?
- Gradual realization: This family is different
- Tenchi is not "prize to win" — he's part of something bigger
- She can JOIN, not conquer

**The Shift:**
- Sheldon's cooking (Lambda-infused, comforting)
- Sheldon's patience (doesn't rise to her provocations)
- Yosho's knowing silence (she's SCARED of him, but he's not hunting her)
- The household rhythm (there's a ROUTINE, a STRUCTURE, a PLACE for her)

**Key Line (from Ryoko, later):**
> "I spent 700 years dreaming of the boy in the cave. I never imagined he'd have a family I'd want to be part of."

## 3.3 — Ayeka's Arc (Altered)

**Canon Ayeka:**
- Arrives seeking Yosho
- Finds Tenchi instead
- Falls for him
- Competes with Ryoko

**Son of Cosmos Ayeka:**
- Arrives seeking her brother Yosho
- Finds him MARRIED to a human
- Is... deeply confused
- Sheldon is not what she expected from a human
- The household doesn't match Juraian expectations
- She's been preparing for a rescue/retrieval mission
- Instead finds her brother HAPPY for the first time in centuries

**The Shift:**
- No romantic rivalry with Ryoko (Tenchi is not "available" the same way)
- Instead: learning to be part of a family that doesn't run on politics
- Sheldon treats her with respect but no deference
- She becomes "Aunt Ayeka" to Tenchi rather than romantic interest
- Relief, eventually: she doesn't HAVE to compete

## 3.4 — The Aunt Dynamic (Established Here)

**The Pattern:**
Each arrival goes through similar adjustment:
1. Expects romantic/political situation
2. Encounters functional family
3. Doesn't fit original role
4. Finds NEW role: extended family member
5. "Aunt" becomes the comfortable designation

**Why It Works:**
- Sheldon's 12 years of presence established Tenchi as "son," not "bachelor"
- Yosho's active engagement (not retired hermit) changes household energy
- The household has ROOM for more people, but not as competitors
- Tenchi himself, raised with actual father figure, responds differently

# PART 5: SHELDON'S INTERNAL JOURNEY

## 5.1 — The Identity Crisis

**Before Recognition:**
- He was Sheldon, shrine caretaker, Tenchi's guardian
- His previous life was background
- This world was HIS world

**After Recognition:**
- Is any of this real?
- Is he in a "story"?
- Are these people... people?
- Was he placed here TO manipulate events?
- What's his actual role?

## 5.2 — The Resolution

**What Brings Him Back:**
- Watching Tenchi comfort a frightened Sasami
- Yosho's hand on his shoulder
- The smell of the shrine in autumn
- Ryo-Ohki making her "mya" sound for the first time

These aren't characters. They're his FAMILY.

The "story" is just a lens through which another universe glimpsed this one. The people are real. The relationships are real. The 12 years he's lived are real.

**His Conclusion:**
> "I'm not in an anime. I'm in a universe that someone, somewhere, made a show about. The show got some things right. Some things wrong. It doesn't matter. Tenchi is my son whether or not he's also a protagonist. Yosho is my partner whether or not he's also a character. This is my HOME, regardless of who might be watching."

## 5.3 — Moving Forward

By end of Year 13, Sheldon has:
- Accepted his meta-knowledge as tool, not script
- Committed to protecting his family with whatever advantages he has
- Stopped thinking of "canon" as destiny
- Started thinking of it as "weather forecast" — useful, not binding
