# LOCATION PROFILE: MASAKI SHRINE
## Son of Cosmos: JumpChain — Primary Location

**Japanese Name:** 柾木神社 (Masaki Jinja)  
**Type:** Shinto Shrine  
**Location:** Okayama Prefecture, rural mountains  
**Real-World Basis:** Tarojinja Shrine

## GEOGRAPHY

### Elevation Profile
- Village base → Torii gate → 320 stone steps → Shrine grounds
- Significant elevation gain through forested mountainside
- Mountain peak above shrine grounds (location of Funaho)

### Access
- Final stop on local bus route ("Jinja-mae" — Shrine-front)
- Single road approach from village
- Stone steps only pedestrian access

---

## STRUCTURES

### Main Hall (Honden)
- Two stories, traditional construction
- Sweeping rooflines covered in gray tile
- Elevated entrance with wooden platform
- Offerings box near door
- "Weight, presence, the particular solidity of structures built to last centuries"

### Temizu-ya (Purification Fountain)
- Located at base of steps, inside first torii
- Stone basin with dragon-shaped water spout
- Bamboo ladles hanging from hooks
- Continuous water flow

### Torii Gates
- Main gate: Vermillion, ~10 meters tall
- Pillars thick enough one cannot wrap arms around
- Paint faded but color retained
- Additional gates along the 320 steps

### Shrine Residence
- Living quarters for Katsuhito
- Guest room for Sheldon (Days 1-17)
- Kitchen, bathroom (ofuro), main room
- Traditional construction

### Tsukino House (Separate)
- Located on shrine grounds but distinct property
- Sheldon's inherited home
- Empty since Yuki's death (1985)
- Sheldon moves in Day 18

---

## SHRINE GROUNDS

### Layout
- Clearing ~50 meters across
- Traditional buildings lining perimeter
- Stone paths connecting buildings
- Gravel borders, carefully maintained

### Atmosphere
- Clean, ordered, maintained with obvious care
- Wind chimes ringing somewhere out of sight
- Forest pressing close on perimeter
- "The particular stillness of moving from secular to sacred space"

---

## THE 320 STEPS

### Physical Description
- Ancient stone construction
- Worn smooth by centuries of feet
- Bordered by stone lanterns at intervals
- Forest closes in along the climb
- Additional torii gates mark transition points

### Ascent Sections (Day 1 Prose)
| Section | Steps | Notable Features |
|---------|-------|------------------|
| Steps 1-100 | Fresh, strong | Ancient stonework observation |
| Steps 101-200 | Breathing deeper | First torii gate, forest closes in |
| Steps 201-300 | Legs feel climb | Stone lanterns appear |
| Steps 301-320 | Final push | Shrine grounds open |

## FUNAHO (The Great Tree)

### Canon Status
- Royal Tree of Jurai
- Yosho's ship, now permanently rooted
- **Cannot take ship form** — this is why Yosho cannot leave Earth
- Located on mountain above shrine

### Presence (Day 1)
> "Outside the window, the mountain watched him sleep. The great tree's branches moved slightly in the night breeze—or perhaps not in the breeze at all. Perhaps in response to something else."

### What Sheldon Perceives
- "The mountain accepted you"
- Sense of being watched/welcomed
- **Does NOT know what Funaho is** (POV restriction)

## HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE

- Masaki family shrine for generations
- Yosho has tended it for ~700 years
- Connected to village community (persimmon messages, etc.)
- Known locally as place that "doesn't keep secrets"

## STORY BIBLE NOTES

### Critical Details
1. Bus terminates at shrine entrance
2. Temizu-ya requires proper purification protocol
3. Katsuhito's apparent age is illusion (~30 true form)

5. Village knew Tsukino's son would return
