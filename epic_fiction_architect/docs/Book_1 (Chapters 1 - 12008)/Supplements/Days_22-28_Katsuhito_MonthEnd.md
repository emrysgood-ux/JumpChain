### SCENE SUMMARY (299 scenes condensed)

Tenchi woke five years old.

Nobuyuki remained through the morning, his departure for Kurashiki delayed to maximize time with his son. The architect sat with Tenchi through breakfast, their conversation a father's attempt to compress weeks of absence into hours of presence.

Yosho observed his son-in-law's attention to the stranger during these interactions. The assessment continued—Nobuyuki processing what he had witnessed yesterday, what he had been told, what remained unexplained.

---

The stranger maintained appropriate distance during the family moments. His presence in the kitchen, his contributions to the household's functioning, but not his intrusion into father-son time.

The boundary recognition continued demonstrating social intelligence that exceeded the stranger's claimed academic background. A theoretical physicist might understand abstract systems; this man understood people.

---

## MIDDAY - DEPARTURE (Scenes 101-150)

Nobuyuki's car waited. The goodbye was extended, the child's reluctance to release his father visible in clinging hands and repeated requests.

"Can you stay longer?"

"I have to work, Tenchi. But I'll be back soon. In two weeks for the assessment."

"Promise?"

"Promise."

The stranger witnessed this exchange from the engawa, his expression carrying something difficult to categorize. Recognition, perhaps. Understanding of a child's need for present parents.

What had the stranger's childhood been? The question surfaced and filed itself for later consideration.

---

Nobuyuki's final words to Yosho: "Watch him. The stranger. He's not what he appears."

"I know."

"But you're letting him stay."

"Because what he appears to be—helpful, genuine, invested in this household—may be exactly what he is. The other aspects don't negate that possibility."

Nobuyuki's expression suggested incomplete agreement. But he didn't argue. The investigator in him would pursue his own lines of inquiry. The son-in-law in him would respect Yosho's judgment in the interim.

---

## AFTERNOON (Scenes 151-200)

The household settled into post-celebration rhythm. Three people again: grandfather, grandson, stranger.

The stranger returned to his project at the Tsukino house, the roof assessment having clarified his immediate priorities. The labor occupied his afternoon while Tenchi processed his father's departure with the resilience of a child who had learned not to expect continuous presence.

Yosho observed from appropriate distance. The stranger's approach to work had become predictable: systematic assessment, efficient execution, quality results. Whatever provided his capabilities, they delivered consistently.

---

## EVENING (Scenes 201-299)

Dinner gathered them. The stranger's cooking remained excellent; the household rhythm remained stable.

"Grandpa, when will Papa come back?"

"In two weeks. He promised."

"That's a lot of days."

"Fourteen. You can count them on your calendar."

The child's calendar—a gift from the previous birthday—had become a tool for managing the separations that defined his family structure. The counting helped. The concreteness of numbered days provided something to hold.

# DAY 25 - APRIL 27, 1989 (THURSDAY)
## **The Nishida Assessment**

---

### SCENE SUMMARY (299 scenes condensed)

## MORNING (Scenes 001-100)

Nishida Hideo arrived mid-morning, his truck bearing the tools and attitude of a professional who had assessed more failing structures than he could count.

The stranger met him at the property with the deference of someone understanding hierarchies: the expert visiting, the amateur seeking guidance.

Yosho observed from the shrine grounds, distant enough to allow the assessment to proceed naturally, close enough to track significant exchanges.

---

The assessment commenced with systematic thoroughness. Nishida's experience visible in his methodology: exterior first, then interior, then structure. Each element examined with the attention that separated true expertise from casual observation.

The stranger followed at appropriate distance, asking questions that demonstrated preparation rather than ignorance. Nishida's responses grew more detailed as he recognized a client who would actually understand technical explanation.

---

## MIDDAY (Scenes 101-150)

The assessment continued through midday. Yosho approached during a break, ostensibly bringing tea, actually gathering information.

"The foundation surprises me," Nishida said. "Built to standards that exceed the era. Whoever constructed this knew what they were doing."

"Masaru's family had resources," Yosho offered.

"More than resources—knowledge. The stone work is exceptional."

The stranger absorbed this information with visible interest. The foundation's quality suggested the house was worth saving, that the superstructure's problems existed above a base capable of supporting restoration.

---

## AFTERNOON (Scenes 151-200)

The assessment concluded late afternoon. Nishida's expression had evolved from professional neutrality to something more complex—respect mixed with concern.

"The main beam is compromised. Three to five years before catastrophic failure, maybe less depending on weather cycles."

"And the remedy?"

"Complete replacement. The supporting structure will need temporary bracing while the beam is removed and a new one installed. Significant work."

The stranger received this verdict with the stability Yosho had come to expect. The news was bad but not surprising; the preparation had included worst-case consideration.

"What timeline for the work?"

"Eighteen to twenty-four months if approached properly. Rushing invites failure."

---

## EVENING (Scenes 201-299)

The household gathered for dinner after Nishida's departure. The assessment's implications required processing; the stranger shared his understanding with appropriate brevity.

"The foundation is solid. The problems exist above it. Repair is possible but will require significant time and resources."

"You intend to proceed."

"I intend to try. Whether I can assemble the resources remains to be determined."

The commitment was notable. A prudent person might have abandoned the property given the scope of required work. The stranger intended to persist.

Either genuine investment in the restoration, or a purpose that required maintaining presence in this location.

Both possibilities required continued observation.

---

**Day 25 Assessment:**
- Nishida assessment completed
- Structural damage confirmed: 18-24 month repair timeline
- Foundation quality exceeds expectation
- Stranger's response: commitment to restoration
- Financial resources unclear; determination evident

The final night of April. The cave remained sealed. The demon remained dormant. The tree-ship remained quiet beneath the mountain.

And the stranger—the mystery that had arrived with spring—had become part of the household's fabric.

Whatever he was, wherever he came from, whatever purpose had placed him here: he was staying. The observation would continue. But the rejection would not occur.

Tenchi had accepted him. The mountain had accepted him. The village was accepting him.

Perhaps it was time for Yosho to move from observation toward something closer to trust.

Not full trust—that required understanding the stranger's true nature. But provisional trust. Working trust. The recognition that actions demonstrated character regardless of unexplained circumstances.

One month complete. Eleven more in the year ahead. Whatever revelations waited, they would emerge in their own time.

Patience had served for seven centuries. It would serve for this, too.
