# PART II: AWAKENING FIELD

**Source:** awakening_field.md

## 2.1 Overview

The fallow agricultural field where Sheldon Tsukino first awakens on April 3, 1989 — the starting point of his new life.

## 2.2 Position

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Distance to Road** | ~400 meters south |
| **Distance to Shrine** | ~3.2 km (via road) |
| **Distance to Village** | ~10 minutes by vehicle |
| **Elevation** | Foothills; gentle upward slope |

## 2.3 Physical Description

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Type** | Fallow agricultural land |
| **Surface** | Ankle-high grass, scattered wildflowers |
| **Condition** | Recently rained on but not yet dried |

### Key Features
- **Road Sign:** "Masaki Shrine 3.2 km" with arrow
- **Cherry Tree:** Pale pink blossoms at field's edge
- **Treeline:** Japanese cedar to north

## 2.4 Sensory Details

| Sense | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Smell** | Green grass, damp earth, distant woodsmoke, sumire flowers |
| **Sound** | Uguisu (bush warbler) — "three questioning notes"; wind; silence |
| **Touch** | Cool damp grass, soft soil, spring breeze (~18°C) |
| **Sight** | Deep blue sky, white clouds, treeline, distant mountains |

## 2.5 Flora: Sumire (Japanese Violet)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Appearance** | Small (2-3 cm), five-petaled, purple to lavender |
| **Blooming** | March to May (perfect for April 3) |
| **Cultural Meaning** | Faithfulness, modesty, sincerity |

## 2.6 Why Cosmos Chose This Location

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Proximity** | Close enough to reach shrine by transit |
| **Distance** | Far enough to require a journey |
| **Symbolism** | Open field = blank slate; must walk toward new life |
| **Privacy** | Rural enough to avoid witnesses |
| **Season** | Spring timing emphasizes rebirth theme |

## 3.4 The Funaho Tree — Hidden Truth

**What Observers See:**
- Ancient Japanese cedar, far larger than natural
- Trunk wider than adult is tall
- "Most present thing in the courtyard"

**What It Actually Is:**
- First-generation Royal Tree of Jurai
- Yosho's partner; permanently rooted 700 years
- Contains power to generate Light Hawk Wings
- Connected to Tsunami
- Named after Empress Funaho (Yosho's mother)

**Effect on Sensitive Visitors:**
- Sheldon feels "something stir in his chest" near it
- Lambda resonance (unconscious recognition)
- Hikaru (cat) comfortable near it (unlike cave)

# PART VIII: ANNUAL CALENDAR

## 8.1 Major Ceremonies

### New Year / Shogatsu (January 1-3)
**BUSIEST PERIOD OF ENTIRE YEAR**
- Largest crowd (hatsumode — first shrine visit)
- Sheldon's Role: Essential helper; proves indispensable

### Tenchi's Birthday (April 24)
- Turns 5 in Year 1
- Family celebration

### Shichi-Go-San (November 15)
- Seven-Five-Three celebration for children
- Tenchi's age 5 = boy's celebration year
- Formal hakama outfit required

### Autumn Festival (Mid-October)
- Masaki Shrine's major annual event
- Mikoshi procession; food stalls; games
- Sheldon's first time seen as shrine family publicly

## 8.2 Obon Festival (August 13-16)

**Most emotionally significant period for household**

| Date | Activity |
|------|----------|
| **August 13** | Mukaebi (welcoming fire); spirits return |
| **August 14-15** | Offerings; family gathered; quiet time |
| **August 16** | Okuribi (sending fire); Kiyone's death anniversary |
