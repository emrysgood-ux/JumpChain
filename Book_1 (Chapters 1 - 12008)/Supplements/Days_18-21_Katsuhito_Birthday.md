# DAY 18 - APRIL 20, 1989 (THURSDAY)
## **Gift Preparation Observed**

---

### SCENE SUMMARY (299 scenes condensed)

## MORNING (Scenes 001-100)

The stranger was building something.

Katsuhito observed from appropriate distance as morning's work revealed the project: a wooden box, small, constructed with joinery that required no nails, no glue. The technique was traditional Japanese puzzle box craft—a skill that took years to master.

The stranger had never made one before. He claimed no woodworking experience beyond what he'd learned in seventeen days on this mountain.

Yet the box was forming correctly. The panels fit. The channels aligned. The mechanism functioned.

---

"A gift for Tenchi," the stranger explained when asked. "The birthday seemed to warrant something made rather than bought."

"The craft is impressive for someone without training."

"The craft arrived when I needed it. As with everything else."

The admission carried the same puzzled quality that characterized all the stranger's self-assessments. He didn't understand his own capabilities any better than Katsuhito did.

Unless that confusion was itself a performance.

After seventeen days, Katsuhito still couldn't determine which.

---

## AFTERNOON (Scenes 101-200)

The box progressed through afternoon work. A carving emerged to accompany it—a tanuki figure, the mischievous forest spirit that populated Japanese folklore.

"Tenchi enjoys tanuki stories," the stranger said. "The trickster who's fundamentally kind."

The observation was accurate. The child did favor tanuki tales. The stranger had noticed, had paid attention, had incorporated that knowledge into his gift.

The attention to detail was either genuinely touching or strategically calculated.

---

## EVENING (Scenes 201-299)

The evening brought Tenchi's visible excitement—the birthday countdown reaching four days.

"Is Papa coming?"

"Papa is coming. He promised."

The reassurance required repetition. The child's anxiety about Nobuyuki's reliability touched wounds that hadn't fully healed.

The stranger observed but didn't intrude. The appropriate response—recognizing family dynamics that weren't his to navigate.

# DAY 21 - APRIL 23, 1989 (SUNDAY)
## **Tenchi's Fifth Birthday**

---

### SCENE SUMMARY (299 scenes condensed)

## MORNING (Scenes 001-100)

The birthday morning arrived with the stranger already in the kitchen. Breakfast would be special—sea bream for luck, red rice for celebration.

The meal's quality exceeded even the stranger's established standards. The presentation was correct; the execution flawless; the intention clearly focused on honoring the occasion.

Tenchi woke to smells that announced the day's significance before words could.

---

Nobuyuki arrived at eight. The meeting between son-in-law and stranger required careful observation.

Nobuyuki was not one of Katsuhito's biological children—he had married Achika, Tenchi's mother, who had been Katsuhito's daughter. The relationship was therefore complicated by biology, by loss, by the peculiar dynamics of a family spanning more years than any human lineage should.

Nobuyuki knew about his own heritage—the diluted Juraian blood that had given him extended life. He didn't know the full truth of Katsuhito's identity, but he sensed more than a normal human would.

What would he sense about the stranger?

---

The handshake between Nobuyuki and Sheldon was brief, evaluative. Both men assessing, both maintaining neutrality, both waiting for more data.

"Father says you've been helpful."

"The household welcomed me. I try to contribute."

The exchange was carefully calibrated on both sides. Neither revealing too much, neither dismissing the other.

---

## MIDDAY - GIFT EXCHANGE (Scenes 101-150)

The breakfast completed to universal appreciation. The gifts followed in traditional order—father's gifts first, then grandfather's.

Nobuyuki's offerings: a construction set echoing his profession, clothes, books. The abundance of parental guilt for absence expressed through material provision.

Katsuhito's contribution: the hero book. The volume that had belonged to his own father—not the birth father on Jurai, but the man who had raised the warrior who would become a legend. The stories inside held truths Tenchi would need when his heritage awakened.

"This book belonged to my father. Now it belongs to you."

The weight of the gift registered on the child's face. The significance understood even if the depth couldn't be.

---

Then the stranger's gift. The wrapped package producing puzzlement followed by delight as the mechanism revealed itself.

"It's a puzzle!"

"The box opens, but only if you discover how."

The child's fingers found the sequence: first panel, second panel, third. The compartment revealed. The tanuki emerged.

"TANUKI!"

The joy was immediate and pure. The gift received exactly as intended.

---

Katsuhito watched Nobuyuki watching the stranger. The architect's analytical mind processing what he saw: a man who had produced craftsmanship requiring years of training after weeks of residence.

The questions multiplied behind Nobuyuki's eyes. The investigation would follow.

---

## AFTERNOON (Scenes 151-200)

The celebration continued through afternoon activities. Nobuyuki and Tenchi building together; the household gathered in celebration configuration.

During a quiet moment, Nobuyuki approached Katsuhito.

"Who is he?"

"I don't know."

"You don't know, or you won't say?"

"I don't know. Genuinely."

---

The conversation continued in low tones while Tenchi played.

"He appeared three weeks ago. He claims to be a physicist from America who died in an accident. He has no documentation, no explanation for his presence, and skills that manifest without training."

"That's impossible."

"And yet he's here. And the mountain accepted him. And he helps rather than harms."

Nobuyuki processed this information with visible difficulty.

"Should I investigate?"

"You could try. I doubt you'll find anything. Whatever he is, he's not in any database you can access."

---

## EVENING (Scenes 201-299)

The birthday cake. The candles. The wish made and kept secret.

The day wound toward its conclusion with Tenchi exhausted by celebration, Nobuyuki preparing to return to Kurashiki, the stranger having navigated his first major household event successfully.

"He's different," Nobuyuki said during their final private moment. "Not dangerous—I don't sense that. But different in ways I can't categorize."

"Nor can I. Seven hundred years of experience, and he's outside all of it."

"That should concern you."

"It does. But concern is not sufficient cause for action. He's been nothing but helpful. Tenchi adores him. The household functions better with his presence than without."

"So you wait."

"I wait. I watch. I learn what I can."

---

Nobuyuki departed with the stranger's mystery added to his concerns. The investigation would happen; the conclusions would follow.

The household settled toward sleep. The birthday complete. The stranger having demonstrated nothing concerning and much that was admirable.

---

**Day 21 Assessment:**
- Birthday celebration successful
- Gift received with genuine delight
- Nobuyuki assessment: suspicious but not hostile
- Investigation likely but expected to be fruitless
- Stranger's behavior: exemplary throughout
- Threat level: unchanged—low but uncertain

The cave remained sealed. The demon remained dormant. The mystery remained unsolved.

But the household was happy, the child was celebrated, and whatever the stranger was, he had contributed to that happiness.

Perhaps that was sufficient for now.
