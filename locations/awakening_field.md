## Narrative Significance

### Why Cosmos Chose This Location

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Proximity** | Close enough to reach shrine by public transit |
| **Distance** | Far enough to require a journey (not placed directly in destination) |
| **Symbolism** | Open field = blank slate; must walk toward new life |
| **Privacy** | Rural enough to avoid witnesses to awakening |
| **Accessibility** | Not stranded; road and transit reachable |
| **Season** | Spring timing emphasizes rebirth theme |

### The Journey as Narrative

Cosmos could have placed Sheldon at the shrine's doorstep. She didn't.

The walk to the road, the ride with Yamamoto, the bus to the shrine base, the climb of 320 steps ‚Äî each stage is intentional. Sheldon must *choose* to move toward his new life with each step. The journey itself is part of the healing.

| Warmth in chest | Scene 1 (first sensation) | Throughout Phase I (Cosmos connection) |
| Spring/rebirth | Field in bloom | Anniversary scenes, seasonal markers |
