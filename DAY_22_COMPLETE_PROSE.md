# DAY 22 — FIVE
## April 24, 1989 (Monday)
## Son of Cosmos: JumpChain — Chapter 1

---

**POV:** Sheldon Tsukino  
**Location Sequence:** Tsukino House → Shrine (all day celebration)  
**Weather:** Clear, 22°C high, 12°C low, beautiful spring day  
**Sunrise:** 5:21 AM | **Sunset:** 6:44 PM

---

## SCENE 1: BIRTHDAY MORNING
**Time:** 05:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Tsukino House, bedroom

Sheldon woke to the awareness that somewhere up the mountain, a boy had turned five.

The thought arrived before full consciousness—the date's significance preceding the day's practical requirements. April 24, 1989. Tenchi Masaki's birthday. A milestone in a life that had only just begun, celebrated by a family that had absorbed Sheldon into its rhythms without asking permission.

He rose earlier than necessary, the day's importance demanding attention.

---

## SCENE 2: MORNING PREPARATION
**Time:** 06:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Tsukino House, kitchen

The gift had been prepared yesterday.

Not elaborate—Sheldon's resources didn't permit elaborate—but thoughtful. A small wooden box he'd found in Etsuko's storage, cleaned and polished during evening hours, containing a set of colored pencils from the village stationery shop. Practical for a boy entering formal education's art classes, personal enough to suggest consideration.

He wrapped it in cloth rather than paper, following the Japanese custom Katsuhito had explained. *Furoshiki*—the art of wrapping that created gifts from the wrapping itself.

---

## SCENE 3: EARLY WALK
**Time:** 06:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Path from Tsukino House to Shrine

The mountain wore spring's best face.

Cherry blossoms had finished their display, but azaleas had begun—pink and purple splashes against the forest's greens. The air carried warmth without humidity, the perfect balance of late April before rainy season's approach. A good day for a birthday. A good day for any celebration.

Sheldon climbed with his gift tucked carefully under one arm, anticipating the joy that children brought to their special days.

---

## SCENE 4: BIRTHDAY BOY
**Time:** 07:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Masaki Shrine, entrance

Tenchi waited at the gate.

Not in his usual clothing—in formal wear that suggested Katsuhito's preparation. A small hakama and haori in deep blue, the traditional attire that boys wore for significant occasions. His hair had been combed into unusual neatness. His face carried the barely-contained excitement of someone who had been awake for hours.

"SHERU-JI! I'M FIVE!"

"Happy birthday, Tenchi-kun."

"I woke up and I WAS five and ojii-chan made special breakfast and tou-san said I could wear the nice clothes and LOOK—" He pointed to his outfit with both hands. "I look like a samurai!"

"You look very distinguished."

"What's distinguished?"

"Important. Worthy of respect."

"Oh." Tenchi considered this, then nodded solemnly. "I AM distinguished. I'm FIVE."

---

## SCENE 5: MORNING CEREMONY
**Time:** 07:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, main hall

Katsuhito had prepared a small ceremony.

Not the full Shichi-go-san ritual—that would come in November—but an abbreviated blessing appropriate for a birthday. Tenchi knelt on the cushion before the altar with remarkable stillness, his usual energy temporarily subdued by the setting's gravity.

The priest's words were formal, traditional, the Japanese carrying nuances Sheldon couldn't fully parse. But the intention was clear: gratitude for five years of health, prayers for continued growth, recognition of a child's journey from infancy toward the person he would become.

Nobuyuki watched from beside Sheldon, his expression carrying the particular intensity of a parent witnessing their child's milestones. Pride, certainly. But also something that might have been grief—for the mother who should have been present, for the moments she would never see.

---

## SCENE 6: BREAKFAST CELEBRATION
**Time:** 08:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, dining area

The birthday breakfast exceeded normal standards.

Special rice with red beans—*sekihan*, the traditional celebration dish. Tamagoyaki rolled into precise spirals. Fresh fruit arranged with artistic consideration. Miso soup made with the finest ingredients from Katsuhito's stores. The meal announced that today was different, that one member of the household had reached a milestone worth marking.

Tenchi ate with the appetite of a child who had been awake since before dawn, his formal attire somehow surviving the meal without visible damage.

"Now presents?" he asked as the dishes were cleared.

"Now waiting," Katsuhito said. "Presents are for after the meal."

"But breakfast IS the meal."

"Birthday dinner is the meal. Breakfast is the beginning of waiting."

The logic failed to satisfy, but Tenchi accepted the boundary with only minimal protest.

---

## SCENE 7: KINDERGARTEN DECISION
**Time:** 08:45:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, engawa

"Should he attend school today?"

Nobuyuki posed the question to Katsuhito while Tenchi played in the garden, his formal clothes now protected by an apron that diminished their dignity considerably.

"The routine matters," Katsuhito said. "Birthdays are special, but school continues. He can celebrate this evening."

"He's been looking forward to this for weeks."

"He's been looking forward to presents and cake. Those will still exist after school." The priest's tone carried mild humor. "Missing one day creates precedent. Precedent creates expectation. Expectation creates disappointment when circumstances don't permit exceptions."

Nobuyuki considered this, then nodded. "You're right. He'll complain, but he'll survive."

---

## SCENE 8: TENCHI'S PROTEST
**Time:** 09:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, entrance

"But it's my BIRTHDAY."

"Yes. And school continues on birthdays." Katsuhito helped the boy change from formal wear to school clothes. "Your classmates will want to celebrate with you. Hayashi-sensei may have prepared something."

"But the PRESENTS—"

"Will be here when you return. Presents do not expire."

Tenchi's protest collapsed into acceptance with the resignation of someone who had lost this argument before. He allowed his grandfather to straighten his school uniform, accepted the small bag containing his lunch and supplies, and trudged toward the path with the exaggerated reluctance of birthday injustice.

"I'll walk him down," Sheldon offered.

"That would be helpful. The bus stop is at the village center."

---

## SCENE 9: WALK TO SCHOOL
**Time:** 09:15:00 JST  
**Location:** Path from shrine to village

Tenchi's mood improved as they walked.

"Hayashi-sensei makes birthday crowns," he confided. "Paper crowns. With my name on them. And everyone sings the birthday song."

"That sounds special."

"It IS special. Yamamoto-kun cried on his birthday because his crown ripped. But Hayashi-sensei fixed it with tape." The injustice of missing presents faded as kindergarten's pleasures reasserted themselves. "Do you think she remembered? About the crown?"

"I think she probably did."

"What if she forgot?"

"Then you can remind her. Teachers appreciate reminders."

---

## SCENE 10: BUS STOP FAREWELL
**Time:** 09:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Village center, bus stop

The yellow bus arrived with rural punctuality.

Tenchi joined the queue of children boarding, his earlier reluctance entirely forgotten. He waved from the bus window with both arms, his enthusiasm restored by the prospect of classmate attention.

Sheldon watched the bus disappear around the curve, then began the walk back up the mountain.

---

## SCENE 11: ADULT MORNING
**Time:** 10:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, main house

With Tenchi at school, the adults settled into birthday preparation.

Nobuyuki retrieved the presents from their hiding place—the wooden sword, a set of building blocks from Tokyo, a illustrated book about animals. Katsuhito arranged the dining room for evening celebration, positioning cushions and checking that the cake remained properly refrigerated.

Sheldon contributed where requested, observing the choreography of family ritual.

"The sword," Nobuyuki said as they worked. "Father insists it's appropriate. I remain uncertain."

"What specifically concerns you?"

"The tradition it represents. The expectations it creates." He positioned the wrapped sword with visible reluctance. "Tenchi is a normal boy. I want him to have a normal childhood. Wooden swords lead to steel swords. Steel swords lead to—" He didn't finish.

"To what?"

"To whatever my father hasn't told me about this family's history."

---

## SCENE 12: LUNCH INTERLUDE
**Time:** 12:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, kitchen

Lunch was simple, the morning's work having created appetite without requiring elaborate meal.

The three men ate together—Sheldon, Nobuyuki, Katsuhito—in the particular quiet of adults preparing for a child's return. The conversation wandered through safe topics: weather, construction progress, Tokyo's current events.

"The house is nearly finished," Katsuhito observed. "Yamada's assessment last week was positive."

"Livable but not finished," Sheldon said, quoting Takeshi.

"The distinction matters less than it seems. Houses are never truly finished. They simply reach states of sufficient completion."

---

## SCENE 13: AFTERNOON PREPARATIONS
**Time:** 14:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, dining area

The cake emerged from refrigeration for final preparation.

Katsuhito positioned five candles with geometric precision—four in a square pattern, one at center. The strawberries had survived transport and storage, their arrangement still pleasing, the frosting intact.

"Yamazaki-san's work is reliable," the priest observed. "She has made our celebration cakes for thirty years. She made this same cake for Nobuyuki at five, for Tennyo at five, for Tenchi's mother at five."

"A tradition."

"One of many. Traditions accumulate. They become the architecture of family life, the scaffolding that supports what might otherwise collapse under its own weight."

---

## SCENE 14: TENCHI'S RETURN
**Time:** 15:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, entrance

The birthday boy returned triumphant.

"Hayashi-sensei remembered!" He brandished a paper crown, slightly crumpled from the bus ride but intact. "And we had cake at school too! A small cake! With one candle because there wasn't room for five!"

"Two cakes in one day," Nobuyuki said. "You're a fortunate young man."

"I KNOW." Tenchi bounced toward the main house. "Now PRESENTS?"

"Now presents."

---

## SCENE 15: GIFT OPENING
**Time:** 15:45:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, dining area

The presents were opened in order of size, smallest to largest.

Sheldon's gift came first—the wooden box, the colored pencils, the furoshiki wrapping that Tenchi carefully unfolded with unexpected patience.

"COLORS!" The pencils were examined individually, each one held up to the light as if confirming its reality. "For drawing! I can draw samurai now! And dragons! And—and—" The possibilities overwhelmed language.

"I'm glad you like them."

"They're PERFECT." The declaration carried absolute sincerity. "Thank you, Sheru-ji."

---

## SCENE 16: THE SWORD
**Time:** 16:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, dining area

The wooden sword was last, largest, most anticipated without being known.

Tenchi's hands trembled as they unwrapped Nobuyuki's gift. The shape had been obvious through the cloth, but confirmation brought gasping delight.

"A SWORD!" He held it up with both hands, its weight clearly surprising him. "A real sword! A WOODEN sword! For TRAINING!"

"Your grandfather will teach you to use it properly," Nobuyuki said, his voice carrying carefully controlled neutrality. "It's not a toy. It's a training tool. There are rules."

"Rules are okay. Rules are GOOD." Tenchi swung the sword experimentally, narrowly missing a lamp. "I'll be the BEST at rules."

Katsuhito intercepted the next swing with practiced ease. "Lessons begin tomorrow. Today, the sword rests in its place. Understood?"

"Understood, Ojii-chan."

---

## SCENE 17: BIRTHDAY DINNER
**Time:** 17:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, dining area

The evening meal exceeded even breakfast's standards.

Sukiyaki prepared at the table—thinly sliced beef, vegetables, tofu, all cooked in the sweet soy broth that children loved. Tenchi ate with appetite sharpened by excitement, his portion carefully monitored to preserve room for cake.

Conversation flowed around topics appropriate for family celebration—school achievements, summer plans, the progress of various projects and interests. Nobuyuki shared stories from Tokyo, edited for five-year-old comprehension. Katsuhito offered observations about shrine life, the mountain's seasonal changes, the rhythms of their small household.

Sheldon participated as family member rather than guest, the distinction that Katsuhito had noted now visible in how the others interacted with him.

---

## SCENE 18: THE CAKE
**Time:** 18:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, dining area

The lights dimmed. The candles were lit.

Five small flames illuminated Tenchi's face—eager, joyful, the concentrated attention of a child confronting the central ritual of birthday celebration. The singing began, four voices in different registers converging on the familiar melody.

"*Tanjoubi omedetou...*"

The Japanese birthday song carried its own particular sweetness, the language shaping celebration into sounds that belonged to this culture, this family, this moment.

"Make a wish," Nobuyuki said as the song concluded.

Tenchi closed his eyes with theatrical intensity, his wish presumably formulating behind the expression of fierce concentration. Then he blew—one breath, all five candles extinguished, the room briefly dark before Katsuhito restored the lights.

"CAKE!"

---

## SCENE 19: CAKE CONSUMPTION
**Time:** 18:45:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, dining area

Yamazaki-san's cake proved as excellent as its appearance had promised.

The frosting was sweet without cloying, the cake itself moist and properly dense, the strawberries fresh enough to release juice when bitten. Tenchi consumed his slice with systematic thoroughness, ensuring no crumb escaped attention.

"Good?" Nobuyuki asked.

"THE BEST." The superlative was delivered through a mouthful of frosting. "Can I have another piece?"

"After your stomach has time to consider the first one."

---

## SCENE 20: EVENING PLAY
**Time:** 19:15:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, engawa

The post-dinner hour found Tenchi on the engawa with his new building blocks, constructing something that defied architectural logic but satisfied five-year-old aesthetic sensibilities. The wooden sword rested where Katsuhito had placed it, visible but untouchable until tomorrow's lessons began.

Sheldon sat nearby, watching the boy build and rebuild, the colored pencils already forgotten in the immediate absorption of construction. This was how children lived—fully present in each moment, the next toy replacing the previous one without diminishing either's value.

"He's happy," Nobuyuki said, settling beside Sheldon. "Completely happy. No shadows, no complications, just pure birthday joy."

"It's good to see."

"I want to protect it. That happiness. I want to build walls around it and keep the world from teaching him that joy is temporary."

"That might not be possible."

"I know." Nobuyuki watched his son stack blocks in defiance of gravity. "But I can try. We can all try."

---

## SCENE 21: BEDTIME
**Time:** 20:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, main house

Tenchi's birthday concluded with the reluctant surrender to sleep.

"I don't WANT to stop being five today," he protested as Nobuyuki guided him toward bed. "What if I wake up and I'm NOT five anymore?"

"You'll be five tomorrow too. And the day after. Five lasts for a whole year."

"A WHOLE YEAR?"

"An entire year of being five."

The magnitude of this timeline seemed to satisfy him. He allowed himself to be tucked in, the paper crown carefully preserved on his bedside table, the building blocks promising tomorrow's continued construction.

"Best birthday," he said as his eyes closed. "Best birthday ever."

---

## SCENE 22: ADULT REFLECTION
**Time:** 20:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, engawa

The evening concluded with adults on the engawa, tea cooling in cups that had been refilled without conscious attention.

"Thank you," Nobuyuki said to no one in particular, or perhaps to everyone present. "For making today what it was."

"Children make their own birthdays," Katsuhito said. "We merely provide the setting."

"The setting matters. This setting—" Nobuyuki gestured toward the shrine grounds, the mountain's presence, the stars emerging above. "This is where he belongs. Whatever I think about traditions and swords and the rest, this is his home. These are his people."

"Including you."

"Including me, when I can be here." The admission carried weight. "I'll return for Golden Week. A longer visit. More time with him before—" He didn't complete the thought.

---

## SCENE 23: DEPARTURE ANNOUNCEMENT
**Time:** 21:00:00 JST  
**Location:** Shrine, engawa

"I leave tomorrow evening," Nobuyuki said. "The early train Wednesday morning from Okayama. Work won't wait longer."

"Tenchi knows?"

"He knows I can't stay forever. He's learned to accept my departures even if he doesn't like them." Nobuyuki's voice carried the particular fatigue of chronic guilt. "Each goodbye gets easier for him and harder for me."

"That's probably how it should be," Sheldon said. "Children adapting while parents carry the weight of absence."

"Perhaps. I'm not sure 'should' applies to family configurations like ours."

---

## SCENE 24: WALK HOME
**Time:** 21:30:00 JST  
**Location:** Path from shrine to Tsukino House

The path down the mountain carried celebration's afterglow.

Sheldon walked with the lantern's light preceding him, the darkness less absolute after a day filled with candles and lamps and the brightness of a child's joy. Behind him, the shrine settled into sleep. Ahead, his house waited in its patient silence.

Twenty-two days since awakening. One birthday witnessed, participated in, made meaningful by the relationships that had formed without conscious intention.

*This is how families work*, he thought. *They grow around you while you're paying attention to other things. And then one day you realize you belong, and you can't quite remember when the belonging began.*

---

## SCENE 25: HOME AGAIN
**Time:** 21:55:00 JST  
**Location:** Tsukino House, main room

The house welcomed him with its familiar silence.

Sheldon prepared for bed with the efficiency of established routine, his thoughts dwelling on the day's celebrations. A boy had turned five. A family had gathered. Cake had been eaten, presents opened, joy expressed in the uncomplicated way that children expressed everything.

Tomorrow would bring ordinary concerns—work on the house, Nobuyuki's departure, the resumption of daily rhythms. But today had been celebration. Today had been enough.

---

## SCENE 26: SLEEP
**Time:** 22:15:00 JST  
**Location:** Tsukino House, bedroom

The ceiling held no answers, as usual.

But tonight Sheldon didn't need answers. He needed only sleep, and the memory of five candles burning, and the sound of a child's voice declaring his birthday the best ever.

*Best birthday ever*, he thought as sleep arrived. *That's all any of us can hope for. Days that earn superlatives. Moments that become memories.*

Tomorrow would be Day 23. But tonight remained Day 22, Tenchi's day, a day worth marking in any calendar, any life, any world.
