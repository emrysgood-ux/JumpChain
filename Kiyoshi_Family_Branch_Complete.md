# THE KIYOSHI BRANCH — COMPLETE FAMILY DOCUMENTATION
## Sheldon's Maternal Extended Family in Osaka

---

# OVERVIEW

Uncle Kiyoshi represents something Sheldon never had as an adult: living family who want to know him. This branch gives Sheldon cousins, their spouses, children he can watch grow up, holiday gatherings, and the mundane miracle of belonging somewhere.

**Key Relationship:** Kiyoshi is Sheldon's maternal uncle — Yuki's older brother. They share the same father (Takeo) but different mothers. Kiyoshi's mother left when he was an infant; Yuki's mother died in childbirth. Both were raised by Takeo and grandmother Hana.

**Location:** Higashiyodogawa-ku, Osaka — working-class neighborhood, post-war housing that's been upgraded over decades. Close to factories, train station, local shopping streets.

**Family Character:** Practical, hardworking, unpretentious. Factory workers and office ladies who raised families through Japan's economic miracle. They eat dinner together, watch baseball, argue about politics, and show love through food and presence rather than words.

| **Born** | June 14, 1935, Kamogata |
| **Age in 1989** | 54 |
| **Relation** | Maternal uncle — Yuki's older half-brother (same father Takeo, different mothers) |
| **Occupation** | Retired (1988); former factory foreman at Matsushita Electric (Panasonic) |
| **Years Worked** | 33 years at the same factory |

**Physical Description:**
- 5'5" (165 cm), still solid
- Wiry and strong from decades of physical work
- Weathered hands, callused palms
- Face lined, especially around eyes (from squinting at machinery)
- Graying hair, kept short
- Wears reading glasses on a cord around his neck
- Prefers simple clothes: work pants, button-up shirts even in retirement

**Personality:**
- **Quiet authority:** Doesn't need to raise his voice; people listen
- **Practical problem-solver:** Sees a broken thing, fixes it
- **Dry humor:** Deadpan delivery; you're never sure if he's joking until his eyes crinkle
- **Stubborn:** Once he decides something, good luck changing his mind
- **Sentimental underneath:** Keeps every letter, every photo; won't admit why

**Daily Routine (Retirement):**
- Wakes at 5:30 AM (factory habit never broke)
- Morning walk to convenience store for newspaper
- Tends small balcony garden (tomatoes, green onions, herbs)
- Afternoon: watches baseball, naps, putters around the house
- 6 PM: Dinner with Haruko
- 9 PM: NHK news, then bed by 10

**Relationship to Sheldon:**
Kiyoshi is Sheldon's maternal uncle — Yuki's older half-brother. Though they had different mothers, Kiyoshi and Yuki were close; he left the village for Osaka when she was 15 but they stayed in touch. He lost his sister to America in 1958, then to death in 1985. He exchanged letters with her for decades and only met his nephew once (the 1980 visit Cosmos created). He thinks about the boy more than he'd admit. When Sheldon contacts him, Kiyoshi will be reserved at first — that's his way — but deeply pleased.

**Speech Pattern:**
- Kansai dialect (Osaka-ben)
- Tends toward short sentences
- "Aa" and "Sou ka" as acknowledgments
- Rarely asks direct questions; waits for information to come

## Yamamoto Haruko (山本 春子) — "Aunt by Marriage"

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Born** | March 3, 1937, Osaka |
| **Age in 1989** | 52 |
| **Maiden Name** | Yamamoto |
| **Occupation** | Homemaker; occasional part-time work during children's school years |

**Physical Description:**
- 5'0" (152 cm), still active
- Warm face, quick smile
- Permed hair with some gray (goes to salon every month — her one indulgence)
- Wears aprons constantly; has a collection
- Energetic; always busy with something

**Personality:**
- **The talker:** Balances Kiyoshi's silence with constant commentary
- **Feeder:** Shows love through food; will not let you leave hungry
- **Neighborhood network:** Knows everyone's business within six blocks
- **Worrier:** Frets about her children, grandchildren, weather, economy
- **Practical warmth:** Not effusive, but makes sure you have a blanket, tea, a snack

**Cooking Specialties:**
- Takoyaki (learned from her mother, street vendor)
- Oden (winter staple)
- Okonomiyaki (Osaka-style, obviously)
- Pickled vegetables (always jars fermenting)

**Relationship to Sheldon:**
Haruko married Kiyoshi in 1960, two years after Yuki left for America. She met Yuki once when Yuki returned to Japan in 1980, and remembers a thin, tired woman who smiled when she talked about her son. She's heard years of Kiyoshi's quiet grief for his sister. She's prepared to mother Sheldon aggressively — he's too thin, does he eat properly, does he have warm clothes for winter? She'll send care packages whether he wants them or not.

Masaru was a child when Yuki left Japan (1958) and a young adult when she returned (1980). He remembers meeting his aunt and her quiet American son who spoke surprisingly good Japanese. At 28, he's close to Sheldon's apparent age (30), and curious about this cousin from America who's returning to the ancestral village.

- **Haruko wants grandchildren** — hints constantly at both married and single children
- **Masaru and Keiko** are focused on careers, not ready for kids

These aren't serious conflicts — just the normal dynamics of a family in transition. The children are grown, the grandchildren haven't arrived yet. It's a liminal time.
