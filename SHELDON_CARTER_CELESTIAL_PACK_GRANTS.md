## CRITICAL FRAMEWORK: HOW PERK KNOWLEDGE MANIFESTS

**Perks do NOT create mysterious abilities Sheldon cannot explain.**

Perks provide **inherent knowledge**—complete understanding of technique, reasoning, and methodology. What Sheldon cannot explain is **where that knowledge came from**.

### What Sheldon KNOWS (Can Articulate):
- **Technique**: The precise how of execution
- **Reasoning**: The why behind each step
- **Methodology**: The complete process
- **Variations**: When to adapt and how
- **Troubleshooting**: What went wrong and how to fix it

### What Sheldon CANNOT Explain (The Cognitive Dissonance):
- **Acquisition**: "When did I learn this?"
- **Source**: "Who taught me? I never took a culinary course."
- **Scope**: "How deep does this knowledge go?"

### Example of Correct Manifestation:
> He knew the eggs needed a figure-eight motion to incorporate air without breaking the protein structure—gentle enough to preserve the foam, firm enough to prevent weeping. What he couldn't explain was how he knew that. He'd never taken a culinary course. Never read about French technique. The knowledge was simply *there*, as if it had always been part of him.

### Why This Matters for Characterization:
A man with four PhDs would **examine** the knowledge, not use it blindly. The cognitive dissonance isn't "I can do things I don't understand." It's "I understand things I have no memory of learning."

This creates:
- Internal tension (Where did this come from?)
- Self-examination (What else do I know that I don't know I know?)
- Measured experimentation (Testing the boundaries of inexplicable expertise)
- Rational coping (Attributing it to "the system" without full understanding)
