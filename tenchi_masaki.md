## CURRENT STATE (April 1989)

### Physical
- Small for his age
- Dark hair, dark eyes
- Energetic, physically active
- Already showing interest in grandfather's "stick exercises"

### Personality
- Curious about everything
- Asks endless questions ("Nande?")
- Trusting of adults
- Misses his mother (died ~1 year ago)
- Loves his grandfather deeply
- Eager for attention and connection

### Daily Life
- Lives at Masaki Shrine with grandfather
- Father Nobuyuki works in city, visits weekends
- Plays around shrine grounds
- Listens to grandfather's stories
- Warned about the "demon in the cave"

---

## THE CAVE AND RYOKO

**What Tenchi Knows (Age 4-5):**
- There's a cave he's forbidden to enter
- Grandfather tells stories about a "sleeping demon"
- The cave is behind the shrine, by the lake
- The key is in grandfather's room

**What Will Happen (Year 13):**
- Tenchi will steal the key
- He will enter the cave
- He will accidentally release Ryoko
- Canon events begin

**Yosho's Plan:**
The stories and warnings are deliberate. Yosho is cultivating Tenchi's curiosity. He WANTS Tenchi to eventually release Ryoko—it's part of her rehabilitation and Tenchi's destiny.

## RELATIONSHIP WITH SHELDON

### Initial Meeting (Day 1-2)
- Curious about the stranger
- Quick to attach (missing mother figure?)
- Calls him "Sheru-ji" (Uncle Sheldon)

### Development
Sheldon becomes:
- Uncle/father figure
- Cooking teacher
- Story listener
- Emotional anchor

### The Transformation
In canon: Tenchi accumulates a harem of competing women
In this story: Those women arrive to find a family already in place
They become aunts, not rivals

## VOICE CONSISTENCY

### Speech Patterns (Age 4-5)
- Simple vocabulary
- Many questions ("Nande?" "Doushite?")
- Mixed Japanese (period-appropriate)
- Enthusiastic declarations
- Occasional mispronunciations

### Sample Lines
- "Sheru-ji! Sheru-ji! Look what I found!"
- "Why does Ojii-chan sweep so much?"
- "Can I help with the food?"
- "Tell me the story again!"
- "Is the demon scary?"

### Age Progression Notes
- Year 5-6: More complex sentences
- Year 8-10: Serious questions begin
- Year 12-13: Adolescent awkwardness
- Year 13+: Canon personality emerges

## STORY FUNCTION

### What Sheldon's Presence Changes
1. **Emotional Support:** Tenchi has a nurturing figure during formative years
2. **Family Structure:** Not just grandson-of-priest but part of household
3. **Cooking Skills:** Learns from Sheldon (Supreme Chef influence)
4. **Confidence:** More secure in himself by Year 13
5. **The Harem:** Transforms from romantic competition to found family

### What Doesn't Change
- Tenchi releases Ryoko (Year 13)
- Canon characters arrive
- Canon events occur
- Tenchi's ultimate potential
