## CATEGORY 1: PERSONAL DIMENSION/POCKET SPACE GRANTS

These grants remain **DORMANT during Year 1** but exist as latent potential.

### 1.1 ID Creation/Escape (100CP)
**Source:** Celestial Tome (Chapter 10 - Movement/Teleportation)
**Status:** DORMANT until Day 366

**Effect:**
- Create "Instant Dungeons"—personal pocket dimensions
- Escape from Instant Dungeons created by others
- Dimensions are empty, stable spaces for storage or retreat

**Year 1 Manifestation:**
- None visible
- Sheldon may experience occasional "wrong door" moments where spaces feel slightly off
- Contributes to his sense of something hidden within

**Post-366 Use:**
- Private workspace invisible to others
- Emergency escape route
- Storage for sensitive projects

---

### 1.2 Room of Requirement (600CP)

- Excellent for testing, training, research, study, or relaxation
- Time acceleration available within (compressed time)

**Year 1 Manifestation:**
- None

**Future Use:**
- Training space where years pass in days
- Research facility that anticipates needs
- Safe haven for family in emergencies

---

## CATEGORY 2: RESOURCE GENERATION GRANTS

These grants provide materials and supplies for crafting and creation.

### 2.1 Arcane Resources (300CP)
**Source:** Celestial Tome (Chapter 20 - Resources)
**Status:** LOW-LEVEL ACTIVE

**Effect:**
- Access to high-quality magically-active materials
- Materials appear when needed for specific projects
- Never runs out of basic crafting components

**Year 1 Manifestation:**
- "Lucky finds" at hardware stores
- Tools that seem unusually well-made
- Materials that perform better than expected
- "I always seem to have just what I need"

**Example:**
> The timber yard had exactly the right grain pattern for the engawa repair. Three different stacks, and the manager couldn't explain why he'd set this batch aside. "Had a feeling someone would want it," he said.

---

### 2.2 Six Demon Bag (400CP)
**Source:** Celestial Tome (Chapter 21 - Supplies)
**Status:** LOW-LEVEL ACTIVE (as toolbox/pocket contents)

**Effect:**
- Always contains material components for any magic possessed
- Adapts to provide what's needed for current project
- Never truly empty of useful items

**Year 1 Manifestation:**
- Pockets always contain the right tool
- Toolbox produces forgotten items that turn out to be necessary
- "I thought I was out of nails" moments

**Example:**
> He reached into his work apron for a screwdriver and found one—the exact size he needed. He didn't remember putting it there. He'd stopped questioning these small conveniences.

---

### 2.3 Elemental Seeds (100CP)
**Source:** Celestial Tome (Chapter 20 - Resources)
**Status:** DORMANT (no conscious magic use Year 1)

**Effect:**
- Condensed essence of elements
- Power enchantments and magical creations
- Provide raw materials for advanced projects

**Year 1 Manifestation:**
- None visible
- Contributes to unusual quality in Sheldon's crafted items
- Foundation for future enchanting capability

## INTEGRATION NOTES

### Dormant vs Active

| Status | Meaning | Year 1 Visibility |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| ACTIVE | Functioning, provides benefits | Appears as natural talent |
| LOW-LEVEL ACTIVE | Subtle effects only | Appears as luck/intuition |
| PASSIVE ACTIVE | Always on, no conscious control | Invisible to Sheldon |
| DORMANT | Exists but not manifesting | No effect |
| COMPLETELY DORMANT | Locked until trigger event | No effect |

### Year 1 Philosophy

All grants follow the principle: **Effects must appear explainable as mundane talent, luck, or coincidence.**

Sheldon experiences these grants as:
- "I've always been good at this"
- "Lucky timing"
- "Good instincts"
- "Must have learned it somewhere"

He does NOT experience them as:
- Magic
- Supernatural gifts
- Cosmic power
- Anything requiring explanation beyond normal competence
