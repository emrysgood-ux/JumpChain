# Nagi

## Basic Information
- **Name**: Nagi
- **Species**: Unknown (humanoid)
- **Occupation**: Bounty Hunter
- **Partner**: Ken-Ohki (cabbit)
- **Primary Continuity**: Tenchi Universe (also appears in other continuities)
- **Role**: Rival to Ryoko, occasional ally

---

## Background

### Reputation
Nagi is a fearless bounty hunter who has earned a galaxy-wide reputation for capturing criminals. Her name is "feared by all criminals" across the galaxy. She operates independently, taking on the most dangerous targets.

### Obsession with Ryoko
Her primary obsession is capturing Ryoko, a space pirate who has eluded her multiple times. This rivalry drives much of her character:
- Sees Ryoko as her ultimate target
- Will not allow anyone else to defeat Ryoko
- Their battles are legendary

---

## Appearance & Design

### Visual Design
Nagi's character design notably resembles Captain Harlock:
- Mysterious aesthetic
- Partial face concealment
- Dark, imposing presence
- High-tech equipment integrated into outfit

### Distinctive Features
- Stoic expression
- Combat-ready stance
- Professional demeanor
- Elegant despite her violent profession

---

## Abilities & Equipment

### No Superhuman Powers
Unlike Ryoko, Nagi lacks superhuman abilities. She compensates through:
- Advanced technology
- Excellent fighting skills
- Tactical intelligence
- Ken-Ohki's capabilities

### Equipment
| Item | Function |
|------|----------|
| Energy Sword | Primary melee weapon |
| Energy Whip | Ranged/binding weapon |
| Advanced Ship Systems | Via Ken-Ohki |
| Bounty Hunter Gear | Tracking, restraints, etc. |

### Combat Skills
- Master of multiple weapon types
- Tactical combat expert
- Ship-to-ship combat proficiency
- Hand-to-hand capabilities

---

## Ken-Ohki

### Nature
Ken-Ohki is Nagi's cabbit companion, mirroring Ryoko's relationship with Ryo-Ohki:
- Same species as Ryo-Ohki
- Can transform into a spaceship
- Intelligent and loyal

### Romantic Subplot
Ken-Ohki develops romantic feelings for Ryo-Ohki:
- This becomes a pivotal plot point
- Affects multiple confrontations between the rivals
- Sometimes prevents fights from escalating
- Creates comedic situations

---

## Relationship with Ryoko

### The Rivalry
Their rivalry is central to Nagi's character:
- Professional: Ryoko is a criminal, Nagi a bounty hunter
- Personal: Deep mutual respect despite antagonism
- Obsessive: Neither will allow the other to escape

### Warrior's Code
Nagi demonstrates honor in their confrontations:
- Refuses to fight an injured Ryoko
- "Prefers to face Ryoko at her best"
- Would rather wait than claim a hollow victory
- Respects Ryoko as a worthy opponent

### The Bikini Competition
Among their encounters, one notably included a bikini competition:
- Demonstrates the comedic range of their rivalry
- Shows they can compete in non-violent ways
- Fan service with character development

---

## Encounters with the Masaki Clan

### Four Major Confrontations
Nagi appears across four significant encounters with Tenchi and company:

1. **Initial Pursuit**: Tracking Ryoko to Earth
2. **Direct Confrontation**: Battling at the Masaki household
3. **Competitive Episode**: Various contests including the bikini scene
4. **Alliance**: Assisting against common enemies

### Unlikely Ally
Despite being an antagonist, Nagi ultimately assists the Masaki clan against their enemies:
- Motivation: Wanted to claim Ryoko for herself
- Would not let others defeat her quarry
- Enemy of my enemy situation
- Shows capacity for cooperation when goals align

---

## Personality

### Dominant Traits
- **Focused**: Single-minded pursuit of goals
- **Professional**: Takes bounty hunting seriously
- **Honorable**: Follows a warrior's code
- **Proud**: Won't accept easy victories
- **Stoic**: Rarely shows emotion

### Hidden Depths
- Respect for worthy opponents
- Capacity for unexpected alliances
- Sense of humor (occasional)
- Loneliness of her profession

---

## Story Significance

Nagi represents:
- A foil for Ryoko's pirate lifestyle
- The concept of worthy rivals
- Honor among combatants
- The comedic potential of serious rivalries
- Ken-Ohki as Ryo-Ohki's romantic interest
- That enemies can become allies when needed
